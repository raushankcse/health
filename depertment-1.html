Wrist Movements
  • Begin with your arm extended in front of you, palm facing down.
  • Slowly bend your wrist upward, bringing your fingers towards your body.
  • Hold this position for a few seconds, feeling a gentle stretch in your wrist and forearm.
  • Return to the starting position.
  • Next, bend your wrist downward, bringing your fingers towards the ground.
  • Hold for a few seconds, then return to the starting position. Repeat these movements for a set number of repetitions.

Hand Circuit
  • Warm-Up: Shake out hands.
  • Finger Flexion: Make a fist, release. Repeat 10-15 times.
  • Finger Extensions: Spread fingers wide, relax. Repeat 10-15 times.
  • Thumb Touch: Touch each finger with thumb. Repeat 10-15 times.
  • Wrist Circles: Rotate wrists clockwise and counterclockwise. Repeat 10-15 times.
  • Cool Down: Gently stretch fingers, wrists, and forearms for 15-30 seconds each.
Start Doing
Stretch
  • Start by sitting or standing comfortably with good posture.
  • Extend your right arm straight out in front of you, palm facing down.
  • With your left hand, gently grasp the fingers of your right hand.
  • Slowly pull your fingers back towards your body until you feel a gentle stretch in your hand and fingers.
  • Hold the stretch for 15-30 seconds, then release and repeat on the other hand if desired.
  • Remember to breathe deeply and never force the stretch.
Start Doing
Strengthning
  • Round Object Grip: Hold a round object (e.g., stress ball) in your hand.
  • Squeeze the object with your fingers, hold for a few seconds, then release.
  • Repeat the squeezing motion 10-15 times with your broken hand.
  • Clay Pinching: Take a small piece of clay and make the suitable shape.
  • Pinch the clay between your thumb and fingers, then release.
  • Do 10-15 repetitions with each hand, focusing on gentle but firm pinching.
  • Rubber Band Stretch:Place a rubber band around your fingers.
  • Expand your fingers outward against the resistance of the rubber band, then release.
  • Perform 10-15 stretches with each hand, gradually increasing resistance as strength improves.
